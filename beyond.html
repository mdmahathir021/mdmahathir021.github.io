Interests
Pencil sketching
It started in school — the wrongness of my drawings bothered me enough to keep going. No colour means everything depends on shadow and texture, which is exactly the part I find hardest and most satisfying. Portraits mostly, sometimes shiny objects where the light does interesting things.
Sudoku
Hard difficulty, under 8 minutes. Meditative in a way that is difficult to explain to people who haven't tried it — completely self-contained, no ambiguity, just logic applied patiently until the grid closes.
Rubik's cube
Solved a 3×3 under 30 seconds in 8th grade. Then it broke, I forgot every algorithm, and confidently assumed I could pick it back up whenever. Currently relearning on a 2×2. It is going fine.
Singing & stage
Limited range, no formal training — but tolerable enough that people have invited me back. Performed on stage at university twice, informal events several more times. Also my primary stress coping mechanism, location non-negotiable.
Anime
Two modes: One Piece, for politics, personal ambition, and a main character whose dream is so large it becomes magnetic — and romance anime, for the modern fairy tale with the guaranteed happy ending. I'm aware the second is delusional. That's rather the point.
Learning random things
No system. If something catches my attention, I follow it until I understand it well enough to stop feeling unsettled by not knowing. Topics vary wildly. The only common thread is that something about it felt worth the weekend.
