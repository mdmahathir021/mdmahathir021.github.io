Research
Thermo-mechanical response of anisotropic MMC panels
Undergraduate thesis · MIST · 2025–2026

My thesis started as an assigned topic and turned into a genuine rabbit hole. Anisotropic metal matrix composites conduct heat and carry mechanical load differently along different material directions — and those two effects don't stay separate. They couple. Capturing that coupling correctly, from first principles, turns out to be surprisingly involved.

I derived the governing PDE system — anisotropic Fourier conduction, plane-stress equilibrium, compatibility equations, and thermoelastic coupling through CTE mismatch — then encoded the full conductivity and elasticity tensors with coordinate transformations for arbitrary fiber orientations, and implemented a 2nd-order finite difference scheme in MATLAB. The solver is currently in final validation against analytical benchmarks, a grid refinement study, and ANSYS Mechanical.

A journal paper is planned once results are confirmed. Target journals: Composite Structures, Computers & Structures, International Journal of Solids and Structures.

AIAA Design/Build/Fly 2025–26
Design Subteam Lead · TEAM MIST · Wichita, Kansas, USA

DBF is the world's largest collegiate RC aircraft competition. I led the design subteam — aerodynamic analysis in XFLR5, wing and tail sizing, aerostructural trade studies. The thing I'm most proud of, though, is a MATLAB parametric sizing script I built early in the process. Every design parameter — span, aspect ratio, tail volume coefficients — was wired together so that changing one propagated through everything automatically. The team stopped redoing calculations by hand. It just worked, and people relied on it.

The 17th and 18th global ranks in the technical report and proposal came from writing I led as primary author. For a team from Bangladesh competing internationally, those ranks mean something.

Twenty-odd years later I still sketch, maybe once every other month, always in 2B pencil, always without colour.
