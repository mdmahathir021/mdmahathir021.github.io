Research
Thermo-mechanical response of anisotropic MMC panels
Undergraduate thesis · MIST · 2025–2026 · Supervisor: Prof. Dr. Shaikh Reaz Ahmed, BUET

My thesis started as an assigned topic and turned into a genuine rabbit hole. Anisotropic metal matrix composites conduct heat and carry mechanical load differently along different material directions — and those two effects don't stay separate. They couple. Capturing that coupling correctly, from first principles, turns out to be surprisingly involved.

I derived the governing PDE system — anisotropic Fourier conduction with full off-diagonal conductivity tensor, plane-stress equilibrium, compatibility equations, and thermoelastic coupling through CTE mismatch — then reduced it to a single fourth-order PDE through a displacement function formulation I developed independently. The solver runs on a 21-point stencil for the biharmonic-type equation, with directional stencils for third-order stress boundary conditions, and is currently in final cross-validation against analytical benchmarks and ANSYS Mechanical.

Thesis defence: 10 June 2026. Journal submission planned as first post-graduation priority. Target journals: Composite Structures, Computers & Structures.

Selected Coursework

Stress-strain relations, Mohr's circle, beam deflection, failure theories. The point where forces stop being abstract and start having consequences for material.
Mechanics of Structures, Vibration & Aeroelasticity
Theory of elasticity in 2D, energy methods, thin plate theory — and how structures interact with the airflow trying to deform them.
Heat Transfer
Conduction, convection, radiation, heat exchangers. Directly feeds into the thesis — understanding heat flow before modelling it numerically.
Computational Fluid Dynamics
PDE classification, finite difference discretisation, stability analysis, pressure-velocity coupling. Where numerical methods stopped being theoretical and became something you implement.
High-Speed Aerodynamics
Compressible flow, normal and oblique shocks, Prandtl-Meyer expansion, supersonic airfoils. The physics gets genuinely strange above Mach 1.
Numerical Analysis
Root-finding, linear systems, ODEs, error analysis — implemented in MATLAB. The course that made the FDM solver in my thesis feel buildable.
Aerospace Vehicle Design
Conceptual to preliminary design: weight estimation, wing sizing, tail sizing, configuration layout. Everything the DBF script was trying to automate.
