Research

Thermo-mechanical analysis of anisotropic MMC panels
Undergraduate thesis · MIST · 2025–2026

Metal matrix composites carry heat and load differently along different directions — and both effects couple together in ways that matter for aerospace structures. My thesis builds a coupled solver for this from scratch: I derived the governing PDE system (anisotropic Fourier conduction, plane-stress equilibrium, CTE mismatch coupling), encoded the full conductivity and elasticity tensors with coordinate transformations for arbitrary fiber orientations, and implemented a 2nd-order finite difference scheme in MATLAB.

The solver is currently in final validation — cross-checking against analytical benchmarks, a grid refinement study, and ANSYS Mechanical. A journal paper is planned once results are confirmed. Target journals: Composite Structures, Computers & Structures, International Journal of Solids and Structures.

AIAA Design/Build/Fly 2025–26
Design Subteam Lead · TEAM MIST · Wichita, Kansas, USA

Led the design subteam for MIST's entry in the world's largest collegiate RC aircraft competition. My work covered aerodynamic analysis in XFLR5, parametric wing and tail sizing, and aerostructural trade studies balancing performance against structural weight and stiffness constraints.

I also built a MATLAB parametric sizing script that automatically propagates any design change through all dependent parameters — no manual recalculation. This turned out to be the most useful thing I built for the team.

The 17th and 18th global ranks in the technical report and proposal came from writing I led as primary author. Final competition rank: 58th overall out of teams worldwide.
